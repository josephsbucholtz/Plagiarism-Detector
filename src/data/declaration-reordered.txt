In the late 18th century, a group of colonies chose to separate from British rule. They believed that individuals possess inherent rights and that governments should exist only to protect those rights with the approval of the people.

Over time, they became convinced that the ruling authority had acted unfairly by restricting trade, imposing taxes without representation, interfering with justice, and using military force against civilians. These repeated actions led them to view the government as oppressive.

Although they had previously attempted to resolve these issues peacefully, their efforts failed. This convinced them that independence was the only viable solution.

As a result, they announced their status as self-governing states, capable of making their own decisions, forming partnerships, and managing their affairs independently. They affirmed their commitment to this cause with strong mutual support.
